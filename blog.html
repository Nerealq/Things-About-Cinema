Dune will be seen at the Venice FIlm Festival 2021

The long-awaited Denis Villeneuve film will land at the Lido on September 3, as confirmed by the contest.

The Venice Film Festival has confirmed that Denis Villeneuve's "Dune" will have its premiere as part of its 78th edition, wich will be held from September 1 to 11. The star-studded science fiction film will be screened on September 3, at wich time we will be able to see the first reactions to this so very long awaited Warner bros and Legendary Pictures production and it's excpected to become one of the best titles of the season.

Adaptation of Frank Herbert's influential novel (very recomenden to read), the film has brought together an impressive cast of stars, including Timothée Chalamet, Rebecca Ferguson, Oscar Isaac, Josh Brolin, Stellan Skarsgard, Dave Bautista, Sharon Duncan, Stephen McKinley, Zendaya, Charlotte Rampling, Jason Momoa and Javier Bardem. The story follows Paul Atreides, a brilliant and talented young man who must travel to the most dangerous planet in the universe to secure the future of his family and his people. Malevolent forces provoke a conflict over control of the supply of the most precious resource in existence, and only those who can conquer their own fears will survive.

We are willing to see this long-awaited film that was suposed to be on cinemas before the pandemic. So maybe we can be comenting the result around here.

A Short Film History

Moving images are everywhere today and we can even imagine a world without video and without internet but this wasn't always like this...

The first motion pictures arrived early in the 20th Century, typically with a running time of a few seconds to a couple of minutes. The first films were released in 1894 with Edison's Kinetoscope, wich was designed to be viewed by one individual at a time. Now, it's so easy to watch things on hbo or on tv but before the 1900, what was the scenario?

"Fred Otts Sneeze" is the first motion picture, produced in 1894 by Edison Studios, with a running time of 5 seconds. Human life was revolutionized by the advent of photography several decades earlier, and now those image were coming to life, This was magical technology and a big deal to people. An luckily we can enjoy cinema in veary easy and incredible ways.
